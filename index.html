The Thesis

Across millennia, cultures discovered practices that quietly improved survival, clarity, and cooperation. Even when the mechanism was unknown, the effect was observed—so the behavior became holy. Today, we can quantify these micro‑advantages, test them, and re‑apply them to modern design.

Formula: a tiny advantage r compounded over n generations yields (1 + r)n. With r = 1% and n = 200, the multiplier is ≈ 7.3×.

Experiments (Open Protocols)

1) Resonance & Damping

Clamp 30 cm squares under equal tension. Tap center; record ring‑down. Compare fundamental frequency and Q.

  • Model: fmn = (1/2L)·√(T/μ)·√(m²+n²)
  • Outcome: wool lowest Q, linen low Q and higher f, polyester highest Q

2) IR Spectroscopy

Capture cellulose vs keratin vs PET bands. Correlate with moisture and odor behavior.

  • Wool: Amide I ~1650 cm⁻¹, II ~1540 cm⁻¹
  • Linen/Cotton: broad O–H, C–O bands 3300–1000 cm⁻¹
  • Polyester: C=O 1715–1730 cm⁻¹

3) Microbial Growth

Wear‑tests or agar transfer. Track CFU and odor ratings over time.

  • Expect: wool & linen lower CFU and odor vs polyester
  • Publish raw data in open CSV
